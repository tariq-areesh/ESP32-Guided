Troubleshooting

Use the cards below to quickly diagnose and fix common ESP32 problems.

Sketch too large

Symptoms: Upload fails with text about "sketch is too big" or not enough space.

Resolve: In the IDE open Tools → Partition Scheme and choose a larger app partition like Huge APP. Remove heavy libraries and assets you are not using, then do a clean build. If you still hit the limit, enable compiler optimizations for size and split features into optional modules.

Board not detected on macOS

Symptoms: No port appears under Tools → Port.

Resolve: Install the USB to serial driver for your chip (CP210x or CH340). Unplug and reconnect the board, then reopen the IDE. Test with another data‑capable cable and port. Check Apple Menu → About This Mac → System Report → USB to verify the device enumerates. If it appears but not in the IDE, reboot and try once more.

"Install the CP210x VCP driver that matches your operating system."

Silicon Labs CP210x VCP Drivers

Brownout detector triggered

Symptoms: Serial Monitor prints brownout messages then resets.

Resolve: Use a stable 5 V source to USB or feed VIN from a regulated supply with shared ground. Avoid powering high‑draw sensors from 3V3. Prefer a short, thick USB cable to reduce voltage drop. If running on battery, ensure the regulator can provide 500 mA or more and the cell is charged.

"The brownout detector monitors supply voltage and resets the chip when it falls below a safe level."

Espressif Brownout Detector

Boot loop due to strapping pins

Symptoms: The board keeps resetting or does not upload unless BOOT is held.

Resolve: Free the strapping pins that affect boot (GPIO0, GPIO2, GPIO5, GPIO12, GPIO15). Do not attach modules or pull resistors that hold these pins high or low during reset. Move your circuit to other pins, then press EN to reset and upload again.

"GPIO0, GPIO2, GPIO5, GPIO12 and GPIO15 are strapping pins used to select boot modes."

Espressif Boot mode selection

ADC2 readings unstable with Wi‑Fi

Symptoms: Analog values from ADC2 change wildly or read zero while Wi‑Fi is running.

Resolve: Use ADC1 channels (GPIO32–GPIO39) for analog sensing when Wi‑Fi is enabled, or temporarily stop Wi‑Fi while sampling. If you must keep Wi‑Fi on, add RC filtering and oversampling to smooth the readings.

"ADC2 is shared with Wi‑Fi and cannot be reliably used while the Wi‑Fi driver is running."

Espressif Docs – ADC Driver

Wi‑Fi connect fails

Symptoms: Connection stays in "connecting" state or quickly disconnects.

Resolve: Confirm you are on 2.4 GHz and the password is correct. Try an open network to isolate auth issues. Set the channel to a mid value, move the board closer to the router, and avoid metal breadboards that shadow the antenna. Test with the WiFiScan example to verify RF strength.

Serial output is unreadable

Symptoms: Random characters in Serial Monitor.

Resolve: Match the Serial Monitor baud rate to the sketch (for example 115200). Close apps that may hold the port, press EN to reset and watch from the very start, and try a lower baud like 9600 if errors persist which can reveal flaky cables.
